about


I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Linguistics at the Univeristy of Southern California. Broadly, my research is interested in how phonological decisions are impacted by psychological concepts, e.g., prototypicality, saliency, & gender. I have recently been investigating sound-symbolic associations, both in how they are represented psychologically and also how they are employed by speakers to resolve visual ambiguities. I am also interested in slang and "internet linguistics," and some side work has looked into how the aforementioned concepts affect combinatorial patterns in lexical blends (e.g., Brangelina).

I am a member of the USC Language Processing Lab
